## What Is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where
scammers trick individuals into authorizing illegitimate transactions on platforms
like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or BHIM. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI
scams exploit the user's own actions — the victim unknowingly approves the
payment themselves.

---

## Realistic Scenario: The "Refund" Scam

**Victim:** Priya, a first-year B.Tech student at a private university in Bhopal.

**Step-by-Step Breakdown:**

1. **Contact:** Priya receives a call from someone claiming to be a customer care
   executive from an e-commerce platform, saying she is eligible for a ₹499 refund
   on a cancelled order.

2. **Trust Building:** The caller knows her name, order ID, and registered mobile
   number (data likely obtained from a data breach or social media).

3. **Request:** The scammer asks Priya to "confirm" her UPI ID and sends her a
   payment request of ₹499 on Google Pay — claiming it is to "verify her account"
   before sending the refund.

4. **The Trick:** Priya sees a ₹499 notification and, believing it is an incoming
   payment, enters her UPI PIN to approve it. In reality, she has just authorized an
   outgoing payment.

5. **Escalation:** Encouraged by her "cooperation," the scammer sends requests
   for larger amounts (₹2,000 then ₹5,000), claiming each step is needed to
   "unlock" the refund.

6. **Outcome:** Priya loses ₹7,499 in total before realizing she has been scammed.
   By the time she calls her bank, the money has been withdrawn.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and college students unfamiliar with payment request flows
- Online shoppers who have recently made returns or cancellations
- Senior citizens less familiar with digital payment interfaces
- Anyone who has had personal data exposed in an e-commerce data breach

---

## Consequences

- **Financial Loss:** Victims lose money immediately; UPI transactions are
  irreversible once approved.
- **Emotional Impact:** Shame, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital payments.
- **Legal Complications:** Recovering funds requires filing complaints with the
  bank, NPCI, and the cybercrime portal — a lengthy process with no guarantee
  of recovery.
- **Data Risk:** Engaging with scammers may expose the victim to further fraud,
  as scammers share "active victim" lists with other criminals.
