# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 5 | UPI & Cybercrime Safety

> **Report cybercrime:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
> **Helpline:** 1930 (available 24x7)

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a password manager
      (like Google Password Manager or Bitwarden) instead of reusing passwords.

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and
      banking apps. Prefer an authenticator app over SMS-based OTP where possible.

- [ ] **Keep your apps and phone OS updated.** Security patches in updates fix
      known vulnerabilities that scammers actively exploit.

- [ ] **Never click on links in unsolicited SMS or WhatsApp messages**, even if
      they appear to come from known brands. Always go directly to the official app
      or website instead.

- [ ] **Verify before you trust.** If someone calls claiming to be from a bank,
      UPI platform, or government office, hang up and call back on the official number
      listed on their website — never the number the caller provides.

- [ ] **Do not share OTPs, UPI PINs, or CVV numbers** with anyone over call,
      chat, or email. No legitimate bank or payment platform ever asks for these.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety (Specific Tips)

- [ ] **Understand the difference between Send and Receive.**
      Entering your UPI PIN always means MONEY GOES OUT. You never need your
      PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to "enter your PIN to receive a
      refund," it is a scam — stop immediately.

- [ ] **Reject unknown payment requests.** On Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm,
      if you receive a ₹1 or small-amount request from an unknown number, do not
      approve it — scammers use this to test if the account is active.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your bank's app or UPI settings.
      Limiting to ₹5,000–₹10,000/day reduces the damage if your account is
      ever compromised.

- [ ] **Check the payee name carefully before approving any transaction.**
      Scammers use names like "Google Pay Support" or "SBI Refund Desk" to
      appear legitimate. Verify it is who you intend to pay.

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Keep your mobile number and Aadhaar details private on social media.**
      Scammers harvest this information to make social engineering calls more
      convincing.

- [ ] **Review your app permissions regularly.** Revoke access to your contacts,
      SMS, and location for apps that do not need them — malicious apps use these
      to steal OTPs and contacts.

---

## If You Are Scammed

- [ ] **Call 1930 immediately** (National Cyber Crime Helpline) — the sooner you
      report, the higher the chance of freezing the funds before they are withdrawn.
- [ ] **File a complaint online at cybercrime.gov.in** with transaction details,
      screenshots, and the scammer's number.
- [ ] **Inform your bank** to flag the transaction and request a chargeback inquiry.
- [ ] **Do not feel ashamed** — UPI fraud is sophisticated and affects millions of
      people. Reporting quickly helps protect others too.
